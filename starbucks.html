Introduction

Starbucks drinks can look similar at first glance, but their calories, sugar, and caffeine can tell very different stories. Some are simple coffee orders. Others are closer to dessert in a cup.

This interactive story uses Starbucks nutrition data to reveal which drinks are strongest, sweetest, and heavier than they seem.

Caffeine tells a different story

The strongest drinks are not always the sweetest

Caffeine doesn't always match our expectations. While sweet, layered drinks may feel more intense, it's the simplest option, brewed coffee, that delivers the highest caffeine, with most other drinks falling into a similar mid-range.

What is inside?

The sweetest drink hides most of its calories in sugar

With 84 grams of sugar, the Java Chip (without whipped cream) is the sweetest drink on the menu. Most of its calories come from sugar, with only a small portion from fat and protein, blurring the line between coffee and dessert.
